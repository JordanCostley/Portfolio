Project: Street Eats Concept and Prototype

Role: Concept, Logo, & Interaction

Duration: January â€“ February 2018

Overview

Street Eats is an all-encompassing food truck app with a primary focus on locating ever relocating food trucks. Street Eats aims to connect an ever-growing market of food truck entrepreneurs with those looking for new unique dining options.

Goals
  1. Create an efficient means of connecting users with food truck options
  2. Convey menus and reviews in an easy to digest manner
  3. Allow users to discover new food venues and events

Initial Survey

To begin our journey we asked ourselves a few questions: Who is our target demographic? What information would someone want out of this app? How many in-depth features do we want to include? After interviewing our perceived target audience we narrowed down features and information potential users would be seeking out when using an app such as ours.

We wanted to offer a complete package that would offer incentives both to customers and food truck operators. We wanted to narrow down our own preconceptions and features that we wanted to fit those which potential users were seeking out.

Features

We initially listed a number of features we might make use of a food truck app. We then narrowed down the options based on difficulty, level of importance, and feedback from initial interviews. We also decided to include secondary uses to gauge inital user feedback during testing.

Competitive Analysis

To get a better understanding of current offerings we examined other applications to gauge what they already offered and what they were lacking. We evaluated information based on our surveys to see which pertinent and vital features were already in place and which were missing. We focused primarily on apps that attempted to offer a solution most similar to ours but also took into consideration other food review apps.

Target Audience

We built personas based on young professionals living in urban cities. We imagine our primary users working and living in a city that offers plenty of night life and events. Our primary user would be a type of person who seeks out new experiences. We would also target secondary users who might be uncertain about the world of food trucks by building a trustworthy environment to discover new dining experiences. Food trucks bring an inherent charm but that does not mean certain expectations in regard to food have to be dismissed.

Scenarios

We considered various scenarios that we would want and expect a potential user to complete within the app. We focused both on primary goals of the app as well as secondary, and tertiary goals to get a more robust experience to narrow down areas in the earlier stages. This also included introducing features that we were considering via scenarios that might be beneficial but not a primary function.

Information Architecture
Wireflow & Prototype

We developed a wireflow for how we intended the user to navigate through our screens. We converted this wireflow into a lo-fidelity prototype which we printed out and conducted user testing with. We wanted to ensure that the app operated in an efficient and predictable manner according to the user's needs.

Testing Results & Iteration

We took the information gained in the initial user testing and concluded that a few areas were in need of adjusting. We focused first on the largest areas in need of adjustment before moving onto the smaller, minor issues. We made note of any interactions that a user attempted to make that did not fit our intended direction and flow of design and adjusted accordingly.

These changes were implemented before conducting a final hi-fidelity test. We were able to fine tune the prototype even further from this testing as there were few logistical issues and the primary concerns were predominantly visual.

Map

Our primary focus was on creating an experience and system that allowed users to easily discover and connect with food trucks. By implementing both an interactive map as well as a list system we were able to cater to various users needs according to their preferences. Since there is no one way that all users will make use of a technology, we made certain to offer as many alternatives as reasonably possible.

Truck Profile

By taking observations and note of other apps that attempt to deliver a large amount of information to the user, we designed a layout with information placed in a clear and easy to navigate manner. We instituted a strong use of hierarchy and compartmentalization to ensure everything that would be needed at a glance would be readily available with more in-depth content following.

Events

We explored the concept of offering events as a means of giving both food truck vendors and users an incentive to use our app beyond the primary goal. This area proved to be a challenge on the side of logistics rather than the simple function of a calendar system. Consideration and planning for these things would need to be considered before fully rolling out this feature of the app.

Takeaway

All in all this process was very enlightening and enjoyable to explore. I find myself with a growing interest in the world of food trucks given their rise in popularity. Being able to see our initial goals and pretenses change throughout the course of developing our prototype was very interesting. Taking various theories and concepts and putting them into effect really helps demonstrate their purpose. It's one thing to conceptually design with humans in mind and another to go through the process of designing and iterating to make a better product.

Ultimately being able to explore new technologies is always enjoyable for me so it was a pleasure to explore the inVision App seeing all that it has to offer. Although we did not get to fully flesh out this app I would love to revisit the concept one day to explore other possibilities. The great thing about design is there is always room to improve and learn.
