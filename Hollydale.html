Project: Hollydale Logo & Collateral

Duration: January 2017 (2 Weeks)

Overview

Hollydale Church is based in Marietta and was in need of a new logo and branding. During a merger the leadership wanted to transition into a more modern atmosphere while holding to their core values starting with their logo. Additional collateral for social media was also to be completed to create an overall atmosphere of the church’s brand design.

Task

The ultimate goal of the rebrand was to give new life to Hollydale. The primary task was the creation of a logo with secondary tasks being collateral in the form of social media content.

Concept

I wanted to create a recognizable logo that could be used in a variety of applications. To create a modern look I started in black and white with solid shapes to create a solution that will last years to come. The plan was to pull from traditional concepts and design aspects within various church communities while providing a modern, clean look.

I landed on the concept of using imagery of the cross, nail, and the letter H to build the base of the design. I used organic lines with varying weights to create the main ‘H’ shape and paired it with a modern vertical typeface with uniform line weights. I wanted there to be a sense of liveliness and upward movement of the Cross ‘H’ as to allow it to breathe and standout from the more structured collateral.

Takeaway

Takeaway: I believe I was fairly successful in capturing a look that Hollydale Church was after. I have always enjoyed branding and logo design and this particular task proved to be an interesting challenge. Given the inherit traditions involved with churches it was interesting to attempt to bring various aspects of these traditions into a modern design world.

Next time I visit a rebranding I would like to do a proper brand guideline to include more content for the client. The wonderful thing about branding is that each brand presents unique design challenges and learning opportunities.
