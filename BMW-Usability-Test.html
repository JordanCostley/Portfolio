BMW iDrive 7.0 Usability Test

Project: Usability Test

Role: Writer & Assistant

Duration: March - April 2019

Usability Test

A usability test was conducted on the BMW iDrive 7.0 system. The purpose of the test was to assess the usability of BMW iDrive 7.0 infotainment system, specifically focusing on interactions through the control knob to navigate interface menus.

Summary

The BMW iDrive 7.0 system was recently introduced into BMW vehicles. It allows the users to interact with the vehicle and access a variety of functions and tools. The main display screen attempts to display the majority of information a user could need and interaction is done via touch, voice, gestures, and/or control knob.

Intro

The usability test was conducted by Kennesaw State University students in the TCOM 4120:02 class, Usability Testing. The tests were conducted on the Marietta campus using a 2019 BMW X7. Two cameras were used to capture the interactions of the users: one placed above, capturing the general actions and the second focused on the screen. A moderator was in the vehicle in the front passenger’s seat and an assistant in the back seat running the camera.

This document contains feedback gathered from users, System Usability Scores, task error rates, time of completion of tasks, and recommendations for improvement. The test scenarios, moderator script, and all survey data will be included in the attached files.

Methodology

Participants were recruited at random on the Kennesaw State Marietta campus. The testing team went around the area near where the vehicle was parked for the test and spoke with students to recruit them for participation. Each test session lasted roughly ten minutes for the actual test, not including introduction time and the debriefing. During test sessions, the moderator introduced the system and asked about the user’s background knowledge and prior use, before introducing the scenarios.

After the test session, the moderator asked the participant a few questions including:

•How would you describe your overall experience with the iDrive system now that you used it to complete a few tasks?

•What did you like most about the iDrive system? Why?

•What did you like least about the iDrive system? Why?

The team members completed the debriefing using a SUS form, a usability scale used to score overall usability of an interface, and Product Reaction Cards done digitally to record the top 5 words used to describe the interface.
